  • Conjugate Transpose: swap rows/columns and complex-conjugates every entry.
  • Ket becomes Bra: ∣ψ⟩†=(ψ0ψ1)†=(ψ0‾ψ1‾)=⟨ψ∣|\psi\rangle^\dagger = \begin{pmatrix} \psi_0 \\ \psi_1 \end{pmatrix}^\dagger = \begin{pmatrix} \overline{\psi_0} & \overline{\psi_1} \end{pmatrix} = \langle\psi|∣ψ⟩†=(ψ0​ψ1​​)†=(ψ0​​​ψ1​​​)=⟨ψ∣
  • For a matrix: A†=(α00‾α10‾α01‾α11‾)A^\dagger = \begin{pmatrix} \overline{\alpha_{00}} & \overline{\alpha_{10}} \\ \overline{\alpha_{01}} & \overline{\alpha_{11}} \end{pmatrix}A†=(α00​​α01​​​α10​​α11​​​)
  • Key Identities:
    • (αA)†=α‾A†(\alpha A)^\dagger = \overline{\alpha} A^\dagger(αA)†=αA†

Hermitian and Unitary Matrices​

  • Hermitian: H†=HH^\dagger = HH†=H
    • Self-adjoint matrices, their eigenvalues are always real.
    • Observables in quantum mechanics are Hermitian.
  • Unitary: U†U=UU†=IU^\dagger U = UU^\dagger = IU†U=UU†=I
    • The inverse of a unitary matrix is its conjugate transpose.
    • Unitary matrices preserve norms

Inner Product​

  • The angle between two vectors ∣ψ⟩|\psi\rangle∣ψ⟩ and ∣ϕ⟩|\phi\rangle∣ϕ⟩ is defined as bra-ket: ⟨ψ∣ϕ⟩=(ψ0‾ψ1‾)(ϕ0ϕ1)=ψ0‾ϕ0+ψ1‾ϕ1\langle\psi|\phi\rangle = (\overline{\psi_0} \overline{\psi_1}) \begin{pmatrix} \phi_0 \\ \phi_1 \end{pmatrix} = \overline{\psi_0}\phi_0 + \overline{\psi_1}\phi_1⟨ψ∣ϕ⟩=(ψ0​​ψ1​​)(ϕ0​ϕ1​​)=ψ0​​ϕ0​+ψ1​​ϕ1​
  • Important properties:
    • Order Matters: ⟨ψ∣ϕ⟩≠⟨ϕ∣ψ⟩\langle\psi|\phi\rangle \neq \langle\phi|\psi\rangle⟨ψ∣ϕ⟩=⟨ϕ∣ψ⟩
    • But ⟨ψ∣ϕ⟩=⟨ϕ∣ψ⟩‾\langle\psi|\phi\rangle = \overline{\langle\phi|\psi\rangle}⟨ψ∣ϕ⟩=⟨ϕ∣ψ⟩​ (Complex Conjugate)
    • The modulus is symmetric: ∣⟨ψ∣ϕ⟩∣=∣⟨ϕ∣ψ⟩∣|\langle\psi|\phi\rangle| = |\langle\phi|\psi\rangle|∣⟨ψ∣ϕ⟩∣=∣⟨ϕ∣ψ⟩∣

Orthonormal of the Computational Basis​

  • The basis states ∣0⟩|0\rangle∣0⟩ and ∣1⟩|1\rangle∣1⟩ are orthonormal: ⟨0∣0⟩=1,⟨1∣1⟩=1,⟨0∣1⟩=0,⟨1∣0⟩=0\langle 0|0\rangle = 1, \quad \langle 1|1\rangle = 1, \quad \langle 0|1\rangle = 0, \quad \langle 1|0\rangle = 0⟨0∣0⟩=1,⟨1∣1⟩=1,⟨0∣1⟩=0,⟨1∣0⟩=0
  • This simplifies inner products enormously when working with the computational basis: ⟨ψ∣ϕ⟩=(ψ0‾⟨0∣+ψ1‾⟨1∣)(ϕ0∣0⟩+ϕ1∣1⟩) \langle\psi|\phi\rangle = (\overline{\psi_0}\langle0| + \overline{\psi_1}\langle1|) (\phi_0|0\rangle + \phi_1|1\rangle)⟨ψ∣ϕ⟩=(ψ0​​⟨0∣+ψ1​​⟨1∣)(ϕ0​∣0⟩+ϕ1​∣1⟩)
  • All corss terms vanish due to orthogonality, leaving: =ψ0‾ϕ0⟨0∣0⟩+ψ0‾ϕ1⟨0∣1⟩+ψ1‾ϕ0⟨1∣0⟩+ψ1‾ϕ1⟨1∣1⟩ = \overline{\psi_0}\phi_0 \langle0|0\rangle + \overline{\psi_0}\phi_1 \langle0|1\rangle + \overline{\psi_1}\phi_0 \langle1|0\rangle + \overline{\psi_1}\phi_1 \langle1|1\rangle=ψ0​​ϕ0​⟨0∣0⟩+ψ0​​ϕ1​⟨0∣1⟩+ψ1​​ϕ0​⟨1∣0⟩+ψ1​​ϕ1​⟨1∣1⟩ =ψ0‾ϕ0+ψ1‾ϕ1= \overline{\psi_0}\phi_0 + \overline{\psi_1}\phi_1=ψ0​​ϕ0​+ψ1​​ϕ1​

Outer Products​

  • The outer product of two vectors produces a matrix: ∣ψ⟩⟨ϕ∣=(ψ0ψ1)(ϕ0‾ϕ1‾)=(ψ0ϕ0‾ψ0ϕ1‾ψ1ϕ0‾ψ1ϕ1‾)|\psi\rangle\langle\phi| = \begin{pmatrix} \psi_0 \\ \psi_1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} \overline{\phi_0} & \overline{\phi_1} \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} \psi_0\overline{\phi_0} & \psi_0\overline{\phi_1} \\ \psi_1\overline{\phi_0} & \psi_1\overline{\phi_1} \end{pmatrix}∣ψ⟩⟨ϕ∣=(ψ0​ψ1​​)(ϕ0​​​ϕ1​​​)=(ψ0​ϕ0​​ψ1​ϕ0​​​ψ0​ϕ1​​ψ1​ϕ1​​​)
  • Basis outer products: ∣0⟩⟨1∣=(0100),∣1⟩⟨0∣=(0010)|0\rangle\langle1| = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad |1\rangle\langle0| = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}∣0⟩⟨1∣=(00​10​),∣1⟩⟨0∣=(01​00​)

The Qubit​

A qubit is the fundamental unit of quantum information

∣ψ⟩=α∣0⟩+β∣1⟩|\psi\rangle = \alpha |0\rangle + \beta |1\rangle∣ψ⟩=α∣0⟩+β∣1⟩

  • where α,β∈C\alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{C}α,β∈C are complex numbers such that ∣α∣2+∣β∣2=1|\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1∣α∣2+∣β∣2=1 (normalization condition).
  • Any normalized single-qubit state can be parameterized using two angles θ\thetaθ and ϕ\phiϕ (real numbers): ∣ψ⟩=cos⁡θ∣0⟩+eiϕsin⁡θ∣1⟩|\psi\rangle = \cos\theta|0\rangle + e^{i\phi}\sin\theta|1\rangle∣ψ⟩=cosθ∣0⟩+eiϕsinθ∣1⟩
  • Key difference from a bit: a bit is either 0 or 1, while a qubit can be in a superposition of both states simultaneously until measured.

    • ∣0⟩|0\rangle∣0⟩ with probability ∣α∣2|\alpha|^2∣α∣2
    • ∣1⟩|1\rangle∣1⟩ with probability ∣β∣2|\beta|^2∣β∣2

    • Probability of measuring ∣0⟩|0\rangle∣0⟩: cos⁡2θ\cos^2\thetacos2θ
    • Probability of measuring ∣1⟩|1\rangle∣1⟩: sin⁡2θ\sin^2\thetasin2θ
    • The phase ϕ\phiϕ does not affect measurement outcomes.

The Bloch Sphere​

Every single-qubit state ∣ψ⟩=cos⁡θ∣0⟩+eiϕsin⁡θ∣1⟩|\psi\rangle = \cos\theta|0\rangle + e^{i\phi}\sin\theta|1\rangle∣ψ⟩=cosθ∣0⟩+eiϕsinθ∣1⟩ maps to a point on the surface of a unit sphere

  • θ\thetaθ is polar angle from north pole
  • ϕ\phiϕ is azimuthal angle around equator
  • ∣0⟩|0\rangle∣0⟩ is at the north pole
  • ∣1⟩|1\rangle∣1⟩ is at the south pole
